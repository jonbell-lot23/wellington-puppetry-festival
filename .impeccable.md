# Wellington Puppetry Festival — Impeccable Design Context

Persistent design guidelines for AI-assisted work on this microsite. Inferred from the codebase, brand artwork, and client direction (July 2026 palette refresh).

## Design Context

### Users

Families, puppetry enthusiasts, volunteers, and local Wellington (Pōneke) community members discovering the festival online. They arrive to learn dates, find free Saturday kids' activities, browse the programme, get tickets (Humanitix), sign up for updates, or volunteer. Context is casual and optimistic — often on mobile, often scanning quickly before sharing with whānau. The site should feel welcoming, unpretentious, and community-owned rather than institutional.

### Brand Personality

**Playful · Warm · Community-powered**

Voice is friendly, direct, and proud of Wellington's quirky creative scene ("Proudly Wellington Funky"). Emotions to evoke: delight, belonging, anticipation, and ease — never corporate stiffness or exclusive arts-world gatekeeping. Hot-pink CTAs signal action; yellow sunburst signals celebration; cobalt blue adds a charming, trustworthy cool note. The Cabaret page is the one intentional exception: moody maroon for an evening, adults-only tone.

### Aesthetic Direction

A **hand-drawn circus-poster** microsite — warm cream paper, radiating sunburst hero, tent-bunting stripe accents, rounded Nunito type (Omnes-adjacent), and festival photography in rounded frames. **Light theme** site-wide except Cabaret.

**Primary palette: Pink + Blue + Yellow** — each family has soft / mid / deep shades (see `src/app/globals.css` `--wpf-*` tokens). Orange and green are demoted to legacy minor accents only.

**Typography:** Nunito (sans, all weights) for UI and headings; DM Serif Display available for display moments. Body text uses warm ink brown (`--wpf-ink`), never pure black.

**Anti-references:** Generic nonprofit templates, cold corporate blues, stark white minimalism, heavy dark-mode defaults, unreadable trendy colour pairings.

### Design Principles

1. **Three families, tonal depth** — Build every colour decision from the Pink / Blue / Yellow shade system (`-soft`, base, `-deep`). Yellow carries warmth and celebration; pink drives CTAs and wordmark accents; blue provides charming contrast and deep section backdrops. Do not introduce one-off hex values outside this system without adding a named token.

2. **Contrast is non-negotiable** — Every text/background pair must meet **WCAG AA** (4.5:1 normal text, 3:1 large/bold). Deep blue sections use **white** text (~10.6:1 on `--wpf-blue-deep`). Hot-pink buttons use **white** labels (~4.8:1). Hero date line uses `--wpf-blue` on the yellow sunburst (~4.4:1). When a "bright" hue fails contrast, deepen the shade — never ship muddy or illegible type.

3. **Imagery leads, copy supports** — Hero logo/artwork, community-day photo, and gallery grid are first-class. Placeholders are clearly labelled until real assets arrive. Sections breathe with generous padding and wave dividers between colour bands.

4. **Tickets and community everywhere** — Primary CTA is hot-pink "Get Tickets" (Humanitix). Secondary paths (programme, volunteers, newsletter) stay visible in header, footer, and inline prompts. Saturday free family day is a front-and-centre message.

5. **Preserve the festival's hand-made soul** — Favour rounded corners, stripe borders, sunburst textures, and warm cream over flat sterile blocks. Motion and delight are welcome when subtle; respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Archived v1 site lives at `/archives/v1` — do not let legacy Birdlife green/orange leak into new UI.

## Assumptions to confirm with the client

- **Blue as the dark section colour** (footer, newsletter, programme highlight) rather than deep pink — chosen for elegance and AA white-text contrast.
- **Nunito-only** for now (no Omnes licence); DM Serif is defined but lightly used.
- **Cabaret maroon** remains separate from the main Pink/Blue/Yellow system.
- **Orange/green** retained only as legacy tokens; bunting stripes now use the primary trio.
